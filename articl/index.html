Origins & History

Taekwondo is a traditional Korean martial art that was established in the 1940s and 1950s by various Korean martial artists combining elements of Karate and Chinese martial arts with indigenous Korean martial arts traditions like Taekkyeon.

The name "Taekwondo" comes from the Korean word "Tae" (foot), "Kwon" (fist), and "Do" (way). It officially became an Olympic sport in 2000. It is not just a fighting sport, but a philosophy of self-discipline and respect.

The Belt System

White Belt

Innocence and a lack of knowledge.

Yellow Belt

The earth from which a plant sprouts.

Orange Belt

Signifies the rising sun and awakening skills.

Green Belt

The plant's growth begins to develop.

Blue Belt

The heaven towards which the plant matures.

Blue Belt (Red Stripe)

Higher level of blue, approaching danger.

Red Belt

Danger, warning the opponent.

Red Belt (Black Stripe)

Proficiency required, preparing for black.

Red Belt (2 Black Stripes)

Final stage before the black belt test.

Black Belt

Maturity and proficiency in Taekwondo.
